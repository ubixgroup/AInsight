This can be done by any means including: print Internet broadcast Only health products that have been authorized for sale by Health Canada may legally be advertised in Canada. These products include: medical devices prescription drugs natural health products biologics and biosimilars veterinary health products opioids and other controlled substances Additional regulatory restrictions apply to advertisements of: prescription drugs opioids and other controlled substances It is the responsibility of market authorization holders (manufacturers and distributors) and advertisers to ensure that their advertising complies with the relevant laws. Preclearance process overview Industry compliance with advertising preclearance is voluntary for all health products except opioids. Preclearance is mandatory for opioids. We strongly encourage all market authorization holders and advertisers to have the materials for their authorized health products precleared prior to dissemination. You may find details about the advertising preclearance system, contact information and roles by consulting the List of Canadian Advertising Preclearance Agencies. Health Canada's role Health Canada is the national regulatory authority for health product advertisements. We: administer the: Food and Drugs Act Controlled Drugs and Substances Act related Regulations oversee regulated advertising activities provide policies to regulate marketed health products develop guidelines for the interpretation of the Regulations set standards for health product advertising materials to help advertisers produce messages that are not: false deceptive misleading Our web-based platform Stop illegal marketing of drugs and devices: raises awareness about illegal marketing of drugs and medical devices educates health care providers on the rules governing health product advertising in Canada provides a quick and easy tool to report suspected misleading marketing practices Visit the compliance and enforcement page for more details. Contact the Section for Transparency and Advertising Regulatory Surveillance in Health Canada's Marketed Health Products Directorate at drug-device-marketing@canada.ca.
